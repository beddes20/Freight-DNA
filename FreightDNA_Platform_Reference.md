## 1. Getting Started

FreightDNA is a sales intelligence and CRM platform purpose-built for transportation brokerage teams. It connects customer relationship management, carrier outreach, lane research, financial performance tracking, and AI-powered insights into a single workspace.

### Who Uses It

| Role | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **Account Manager (AM)** | Day-to-day customer relationships, touchpoint logging, lane coverage |
| **National Account Manager (NAM)** | Team oversight, coaching AMs, strategic account growth |
| **Director** | Portfolio-level visibility, rate exposure, team performance |
| **Sales / Sales Director** | Prospecting, pipeline management, new business development |
| **Logistics Manager (LM)** | Carrier outreach, lane capacity, procurement execution |
| **Logistics Coordinator (LC)** | Operational support, coordinator workflows |
| **Admin** | Full system access, user management, data uploads, configuration |

### Navigation

The left sidebar organizes features into four sections:

- **Navigation** - Core daily tools (Dashboard, Customers, Tasks, Goals, 1:1s)
- **Pipeline** - Sales pipeline and RFP tracking
- **Lane Tools** - Lane intelligence, carrier outreach, and procurement
- **Admin / Team** - User management, financials, and analytics

The sidebar can be collapsed to icon-only mode using the toggle button at the top. Hover over icons for tooltips when collapsed.

## 2. Dashboard

Your command center. The dashboard adapts to your role and shows the most relevant information for your day.

### What You See

**Everyone sees:**
- **Today's Briefing** - Tasks due/overdue today, at-risk accounts, contacts needing attention, and unread notifications
- **Recently Visited** - The last 8 companies you viewed, with their current momentum status
- **Pinned Accounts** - Your personal watchlist of up to 10 starred accounts
- **Goal Progress** - Current progress against your active goals with pace indicators
- **NBA Cards** - AI-generated recommended next actions (top 5 for reps, up to 200 for directors)

**Account Managers additionally see:**
- **My Trending Accounts** - Accounts with significant margin shifts vs. their 3-month average
- **My Activity Cards** - KPI tiles for Relationships Moved, Meaningful Conversations, New Contacts, and Touches Today
- **Market Pulse** - Top 3 markets affecting your specific accounts

**NAMs additionally see:**
- **Team Activity Metrics** - Aggregated KPIs across all your AMs
- **AM Margin Metrics** - Progress bars for each AM against their margin goals
- **AM Activity Snapshot** - Comparison of touches, cold accounts, and open tasks across the team
- **Accounts Needing Attention** - Accounts with 21+ days without a touchpoint

**Directors additionally see:**
- **Portfolio Rate Exposure** - Book-of-business rates vs. market (Above/At/Below) with estimated over-market spend
- **Rep Rate Positioning** - Leaderboard ranking reps by pricing relative to market
- **Director Heat Summary** - Hot/Warm/Cool lane counts across the organization

### Common Use Cases

- Start your day by reviewing Today's Briefing for immediate priorities
- Check NBA cards to see what the system recommends you do next
- Glance at Pinned Accounts to monitor your key watchlist
- Review goal pace to know if you're on track for the week/month

### How to Pin an Account

1. Navigate to any customer card or company detail page
2. Click the star/pin icon
3. The account appears in your Pinned Accounts dashboard portlet
4. Maximum 10 pinned accounts per user

## 3. Customers (CRM)

The Customers page is your primary view of all active accounts.

### What It Does

Displays a searchable, filterable list of every company in the system with real-time performance data alongside relationship health indicators.

### Key Information Shown

- Company name and assigned rep
- Total Loads, Spot Loads, Margin ($), and Margin (%)
- Momentum Score band (At Risk, Stable, Growth Ready, Primed to Grow)
- Last touchpoint date and recency indicator
- Contact suggestion count badge (new contacts detected from email)

### Step-by-Step: Finding and Filtering Accounts

1. Open **Customers** from the sidebar
2. Use the search bar to find accounts by name
3. Apply filters:
   - **Assigned Rep** - Filter to your accounts or a specific team member
   - **Industry** - Narrow by industry vertical
   - **Touchpoint Recency** - "Not touched this month," "Not touched in 7 days," etc.
   - **Shipping Mode** - LTL, FTL, Intermodal, etc.
   - **Momentum Band** - Focus on At Risk or Growth Ready accounts
4. Save your filter combination as a **Saved View** (up to 10 views) for quick access later

### Company Detail Page

Clicking any account opens a multi-tab detail view:

**Overview Tab:**
- Primary contact card with quick-call/email actions
- YTD, Last Month, and This Month performance grid (Loads, Margin, Spot vs. Contract)
- NBA cards specific to this account
- Market Share visualization

**Intel Tab:**
- Portal credentials (TMS URL, username, password) with copy-to-clipboard buttons
- Tendering Style, Spot Process, and Account Quirks
- Onboarding milestones checklist
- Financial aliases for data matching
- Shared reps and territory assignments
- Rate positioning panel (SONAR-driven market comparison)

**People Tab:**
- Contact list and org chart (see Section 4)
- Geographic lane pattern ownership per contact
- Contact suggestion badges for new contacts detected from email

**Touchpoints Tab:**
- Full history of all logged interactions
- Callouts/Trends feed for team collaboration
- Unified activity timeline

**RFPs Tab:**
- All RFPs linked to this account with status and lane counts
- Win/loss track record

### Common Use Cases

- Pre-call prep: Open the Intel tab to review portal info, quirks, and recent touchpoints
- Account review: Check the Overview tab for financial trends and open NBAs
- Relationship mapping: Use the People tab org chart to find coverage gaps
- Handoff: Review Shared Reps and handoff notes when covering for a colleague

## 4. Contacts & Org Charts

### What It Does

Manages the people at each customer account — who they are, what they control, and how deep your relationship runs.

### Key Terminology

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **1st Base** | Initial contact made, basic relationship |
| **2nd Base** | Regular communication, some trust built |
| **3rd Base** | Strong relationship, strategic conversations |
| **Home Run** | Champion-level contact, mutual trust and business impact |
| **Relationship Base** | The current level of your relationship with a specific contact |

### What's Tracked Per Contact

- Name, title, email, phone
- Relationship Base (1st through Home Run)
- Reports-to hierarchy (for org chart)
- Lanes and regions they control
- Freight spend influence
- Role type and primary contact designation
- Spot bidding process notes and next steps

### Step-by-Step: Adding a Contact

1. Navigate to the Company Detail page
2. Go to the **People** tab
3. Click **Add Contact**
4. Fill in name, title, email, phone
5. Set the Relationship Base
6. Optionally assign who they report to (builds the org chart)
7. Add lanes/regions they're responsible for
8. Save

### Org Chart

The org chart visually displays the reporting hierarchy at a customer account. Each node shows:
- Contact name and title
- Current Relationship Base (color-coded)
- Annual freight spend they influence
- Regions/lanes they control

Use the org chart to identify:
- **Single-thread risk** - Only one contact at the account (the system flags this)
- **Coverage gaps** - No contacts at the decision-maker level
- **Advancement opportunities** - Contacts ready to move to the next base

### Contact Suggestions

The system automatically detects new people mentioned in email threads linked to an account. These appear as suggestions on the People tab with a count badge visible in the Customers list. You can accept (creates the contact) or ignore each suggestion.

### Copy to Clipboard

Email addresses and phone numbers have copy buttons for quick access in both the contact list and org chart views.

## 5. Touchpoints

### What It Does

Tracks every interaction you have with customer contacts — calls, emails, texts, and site visits. Touchpoints are the heartbeat of your Momentum Score.

### How to Log a Touchpoint

**Option 1: Floating Action Button (FAB)**
- The "Log Touch" button sits in the bottom-right corner of every page (except the dashboard)
- Click it to open the logging dialog

**Option 2: Keyboard Shortcut**
- Press **Shift+T** from anywhere in the app (as long as you're not typing in a text field)

**Option 3: From a Company Page**
- When you're on a Company Detail page, the dialog auto-fills with that company's information

### Step-by-Step: Logging a Touchpoint

1. Trigger the dialog (FAB, Shift+T, or from a company page)
2. Search for and select the **Contact** you spoke with
3. Choose the **Touch Type**: Call, Email, Text, or Site Visit
4. Toggle **Meaningful Conversation** if this was a substantive interaction that moved the relationship forward
5. Rate the **Call Vibe**: Great, Neutral, or Cold
6. Add **Notes** about what was discussed
7. Save

### What Happens After Logging

- The Momentum Score for that account recalculates
- Progress on touchpoint-related Goals auto-updates
- NBA cards may resolve or refresh based on the new activity
- AI scans your notes for competitor mentions, follow-up commitments, and key intel
- If a follow-up is detected, the system may prompt you to create a task

### Touchpoint History

Admins and managers can review all touchpoints across the organization via **Touchpoint History** in the Admin/Team section. Filter by rep, company, date range, or touch type.

## 6. Momentum Score

### What It Does

Automatically calculates a 0-100 health score for every account based on your relationship activity, contact depth, financial performance, and email signals. It answers the question: "How healthy is this account right now?"

### Momentum Bands

| Score | Band | What It Means |
|-------|------|---------------|
| 76-100 | **Primed to Grow** (Green) | Strong engagement, multiple contacts, active freight — ready for expansion |
| 51-75 | **Growth Ready** (Blue) | Good foundation, consistent activity — push for deeper penetration |
| 26-50 | **Stable** (Amber) | Adequate but not thriving — needs more attention to avoid slipping |
| 0-25 | **At Risk** (Red) | Low engagement, stale contact, declining freight — needs immediate action |

### What Drives the Score

**Positive Factors:**
- Recent touchpoints (today = maximum points, decays rapidly over business days)
- Touchpoint frequency over the last 10 business days
- Meaningful conversations in the last 30 days
- Contacts at 3rd Base or Home Run level
- Multiple contacts across different levels
- High YTD load volume
- Active RFPs
- Multiple lane corridors

**Negative Factors (Penalties):**
- No touch in 7+ business days (-10 points)
- No meaningful conversation in 90 days (-7 points)
- No senior-level contacts (-5 points)
- Overdue tasks (-3 points)
- Stalled email threads (-2 each, max -4)
- Service complaints detected in emails (-3 each, max -6)

### Momentum Drop Notifications

- **Real-time**: If an account drops a band (e.g., Growth Ready to Stable), you get an in-app notification immediately
- **Weekly digest**: Every Monday at 7:00 AM CT, you receive a summary of all band changes across your accounts

### How to View Score Details

1. Click any Momentum badge on a customer card
2. The score drawer opens showing the exact breakdown
3. Review each driver (positive factors and penalties)
4. Take action on the biggest penalties first

## 7. Tasks

### What It Does

A unified task management system for tracking follow-ups, assignments, and deadlines across all your accounts.

### Types of Tasks

- **Manual Tasks** - Created by you or assigned to you by a manager
- **Auto-generated Tasks** - Created by the system (e.g., RFP Coverage Review, follow-up from touchpoint notes)
- **Procurement Tasks** - Special lane-capacity tasks with carrier outreach tracking

### Step-by-Step: Creating a Task

1. Open the **Tasks** page from the sidebar (or click "New Task" from any account)
2. Enter a title and description
3. Set a **Due Date**
4. **Assign** to yourself or a team member
5. Optionally link to a Company and/or Contact
6. Save

### Task Views

- **List View** - Grouped by status: Overdue (red), On Time, Completed
- **Calendar View** - Visual layout of tasks by due date

### Bulk Actions

On the Tasks page, you can select multiple tasks and:
- **Mark Complete** - Resolve several tasks at once
- **Reassign** - Move tasks to a different team member

### Permissions

- Admins see all tasks across the organization
- Directors/NAMs see their team's tasks
- AMs/LMs see tasks they created or are assigned to

## 8. Goals

### What It Does

Track KPIs with automatic progress measurement. Goals can be weekly, monthly, or custom periods.

### Available Metrics

| Metric | How It's Tracked |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Touchpoints** | Auto-counted from logged touches |
| **Meaningful Conversations** | Auto-counted from touches flagged as meaningful |
| **New Contacts** | Auto-counted from contacts added in the period |
| **Margin ($)** | Auto-calculated from uploaded financial data |
| **Loads Booked** | Auto-counted from financial data |
| **Custom** | Manually updated by the user |

### Pace Indicators

The system calculates whether you're ahead, on track, or behind pace based on how much time has elapsed in the goal period:
- **Ahead of Pace** (green) - Progressing faster than needed
- **On Track** (blue) - Right where you should be
- **Behind Pace** (red) - Need to pick up activity

### Collaboration

Goals include a comment thread where managers can provide coaching notes and reps can share updates. When you hit 100%, the system celebrates with a confetti animation.

## 9. Launchpad (Prospects)

### What It Does

Manages the sales pipeline from initial lead through first load won. This is where new business development lives.

### Who Uses It

Sales, Sales Directors, and Admins.

### Pipeline Stages

| Stage | What Happens |
|-------|-------------|
| **New Lead** | Initial identification of a potential customer |
| **Intro Scheduled** | First meeting is on the calendar |
| **Intro Completed** | Initial conversation has happened |
| **Follow Up** | Active follow-up and relationship building |
| **Opportunity Sent** | Formal proposal or pricing submitted |
| **First Load Won** | First shipment booked — convert to active customer |
| **Lost** | Did not win the business |
| **Disqualified** | Not a fit for our services |

### Step-by-Step: Working a Prospect

1. Open **Launchpad** from the sidebar
2. Add a new prospect with company name and estimated freight spend
3. Drag the prospect card through stages as the relationship progresses (Kanban view) or update in the table view
4. Log opportunities within the prospect (Single Lane, Multi-Lane, RFP, etc.)
5. When the first load is won, convert the prospect to an active **Customer** account

### Pipeline Analytics

The Analytics tab shows:
- Funnel conversion rates between stages
- Days in stage (bottleneck detection)
- Win/Loss analysis with reason categories
- Rep comparison leaderboard

### Ownership Requests

If you want to take over a prospect owned by someone else:
1. Click "Request Ownership" on the prospect
2. Provide a reason
3. An admin reviews and approves/denies the request

### Stale Prospect Detection

Prospects with no activity in 14+ days are automatically flagged as "Stale" to prevent leads from going cold.

## 10. Top Opportunities

### What It Does

Automatically identifies your best growth opportunities by cross-referencing historical delivery data with active RFP lanes.

### How It Works

The system finds "Hot Zones" — locations where you frequently deliver trucks (5+ loads/week) that are near pickup origins in uploaded RFPs. This means you already have trucks in the area and can offer competitive pricing.

### Common Use Cases

- Identify which RFP lanes align with your existing network
- Prioritize bids where you have a natural capacity advantage
- Create follow-up tasks directly from opportunity matches

## 11. RFP & Awards

### What It Does

Manages the full lifecycle of Requests for Proposal — from upload and lane research through award and procurement.

### Key Terminology

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **RFP** | Request for Proposal — a customer's formal request for carrier pricing |
| **Lane** | A specific origin-to-destination freight route |
| **Award** | Lanes you've won from an RFP |
| **Research Task** | A lane that needs contact assignment and carrier research |
| **High-Volume Lane** | A lane with 50+ annual loads, flagged for priority research |

### Step-by-Step: RFP Lifecycle

**1. Upload the RFP**
1. Go to **RFP & Awards** in the Pipeline section
2. Click **New RFP**
3. Select the customer company
4. Upload the Excel/CSV file
5. The system uses AI to map columns (Origin, Destination, Volume, Equipment)
6. Review and confirm the lane extraction
7. The system auto-creates a "Coverage Review" task

**2. Research the Lanes**
1. Go to **Lane Intelligence** (Research Tasks tab)
2. Filter for the new RFP's lanes
3. For each high-volume lane:
   - Assign a contact (decision-maker at the customer)
   - Add carrier research notes
   - Mark as "Researched" when complete

**3. Submit Pricing**
- Use the lane data and your carrier network to build competitive pricing
- Track the RFP status (Open, Submitted, Awarded, Lost)

**4. Convert to Award**
1. When you win, click **Convert to Award** on the RFP
2. Select which specific lanes were awarded
3. The system creates an Award record

**5. Execute (Procurement)**
1. From the Award card, click **Generate Procurement Tasks**
2. One task is created per qualifying lane
3. Work each lane through the Lane Work Queue or My Procurement page

### RFP Calendar

A visual calendar showing all RFP deadlines and task due dates. Helps ensure nothing falls through the cracks during busy bid seasons.

## 13. Lane Work Queue (LWQ)

### What It Does

Your primary workspace for building carrier capacity on recurring lanes. It transforms raw TMS shipping data into an actionable, prioritized work list.

### Who Uses It

Account Managers, Logistics Managers, and anyone responsible for ensuring lanes have adequate carrier coverage.

### Key Terminology

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Recurring Lane** | A lane that moves freight regularly (identified from financial data) |
| **Lane Score** | 0-100 priority score based on volume, consistency, margin, and strategic importance |
| **Bench** | The list of carriers you've contacted for a specific lane |
| **Coverage Profile** | Assessment of how well a lane is covered: Stable, Watch, or No History |
| **Carrier Contacted Threshold** | Number of carriers you need to reach out to (default: 3) before a lane is resolved |

### LWQ Buckets

| Bucket | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Unassigned** | Lanes with no owner — need someone to claim them |
| **No Contactable** | Assigned, but no carriers in the bench have valid contact info |
| **Assigned Untouched** | Assigned and contactable, but no outreach has been logged yet |
| **In Progress** | Outreach has started but hasn't reached the completion threshold |

### Step-by-Step: Working the Lane Work Queue

**1. Claim or Get Assigned Lanes**
- Self-assign from the Unassigned bucket, or your manager assigns lanes to you

**2. Open a Lane**
- Click any lane to open the **Carrier Outreach Panel**

**3. Review Coverage**
- Check the Coverage Profile (Stable/Watch/No History)
- Review ranked carrier suggestions

**4. Select Carriers**
- The system ranks carriers by fit:
  - **Exact match** - Carrier has hauled this exact lane before
  - **Nearby** - Carrier has hauled similar corridors
  - **Regional** - Carrier operates in the right geography
- Select 3-5 carriers to contact

**5. Choose a Template & Send**
- Pick an outreach template (Quick Capacity Check, Steady Lane Ask, etc.)
- Variables like origin, destination, and carrier name auto-fill
- Customize the message if needed
- Click **Send** — emails go out via Outlook with Reply-To set to your ops mailbox

**6. Manage Responses**
- Carrier replies are tracked and classified (Available Now, Maybe Later, Not a Fit)
- Hot replies show a pulsing green indicator on the lane
- Take action on positive responses immediately

**7. Resolve the Lane**
- Once you've contacted the threshold number of carriers (usually 3), the lane is resolved and snoozed for 30 days

### Lane Scoring Factors

| Factor | Weight | What It Measures |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| Consistency | 25 pts | How regularly the lane runs |
| Volume | 20 pts | Average weekly loads |
| Confidence | 15 pts | System confidence the lane is truly recurring |
| Strategic Tier | 15 pts | Customer importance based on freight spend |
| No Preferred Carrier | 15 pts | Bonus for lanes without a primary carrier |
| Margin Signal | 10 pts | Better scores for higher-margin lanes |
| Volatility Penalty | -10 pts | Penalty for erratic, unpredictable volume |

## 15. Carrier Hub

### What It Does

The central intelligence layer for managing carrier relationships. Think of it as a CRM specifically for your carrier partners.

### Who Uses It

Admins, Directors, NAMs, and Logistics Managers.

### Key Features

**Carrier Directory**
- Search by name, MC/DOT number, or contact info
- Filter by status (Active, Flagged, Do Not Use), equipment type, and contact readiness
- View aggregated stats: total loads hauled, proven lanes, last outreach date

**Carrier Profile Tabs:**

| Tab | What It Shows |
|-----|--------------|
| **Overview** | Legal name, MC/DOT, status, internal tags/notes |
| **Intelligence** | History snapshot (top lanes), declared preferences, AI-detected lane suggestions |
| **Best Lanes Right Now** | Active lanes scored against this carrier's profile (0-100% Fit Score) |
| **Contacts** | Dispatchers, sales reps, after-hours contacts with preferred communication method |
| **Claimed Lanes** | Lanes the carrier says they want (Preferred or Avoid) |
| **Proven History** | Lanes the carrier has actually hauled (from TMS data) |
| **Activity** | Chronological outreach log and TMS load history |

### Carrier Reliability Score

A 0-100 score that helps you vet carriers quickly:

| Component | Weight | What It Measures |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Reply Rate | 40% | % of outreach that received a reply |
| Hard Commitment Rate | 30% | % of offers that resulted in a firm commitment |
| Positive Outcome Rate | 30% | % of contacts that resulted in "Available" status |

**Color coding:**
- Green (70+) - Reliable partner
- Amber (40-69) - Inconsistent, proceed with awareness
- Red (below 40) - Low reliability, consider alternatives

### Step-by-Step: Vetting a Carrier

1. Open **Carrier Hub** from the sidebar
2. Search for the carrier by name or MC number
3. Check their **Reliability Score** for a quick health check
4. Review the **Proven History** tab to confirm they've actually hauled your lanes
5. Check **Contacts** to find the right person to call
6. Review **Activity** for recent communication history
7. Use **Best Lanes Right Now** to see which of your open lanes fit this carrier

## 16. Conversations Inbox

### What It Does

Centralized management of email threads with carriers and customers. Ensures nothing falls through the cracks by tracking who owns each conversation and whether it's overdue.

### Key Concepts

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Waiting on Us** | The other party replied — ball is in our court |
| **Waiting on Them** | We sent a message — waiting for their response |
| **Resolved** | Thread is complete, no more action needed |
| **Overdue** | We've exceeded the SLA for response time |

### Quick View Tabs

| Tab | What It Shows |
|-----|--------------|
| **Waiting on Me** | Threads you own that need your response |
| **Unowned** | New inbound threads that need someone to claim them |
| **High Priority** | Urgent threads (4-hour SLA) |
| **All** | Full filterable list of all threads |

### Priority & SLA

| Priority | Response SLA |
|----------|-------------|
| **High** | 4 hours |
| **Normal** | 24 hours |
| **Low** | No SLA deadline |

Overdue threads show a red badge and sort to the top of your list.

### Step-by-Step: Managing a Conversation

1. Open **Conversations** from the sidebar
2. Check the **Waiting on Me** tab first
3. Click a thread to view the full email history
4. Respond to the email
5. The system auto-updates the state to "Waiting on Them"
6. When the conversation is complete, mark as **Resolved**

### Sidebar Badge

The sidebar shows a live count of threads waiting on you, polling every 90 seconds.

## 17. Email Intelligence

### What It Does

Analyzes inbound customer and carrier emails using AI to detect intent signals — pricing requests, urgency, objections, new opportunities, and competitive mentions. Surfaces patterns that help you act faster and understand win/loss trends.

### Who Uses It

Admins, Directors, NAMs, and Sales Directors.

### Four Tabs

**Urgency Tracker**
- Shows unresponded urgency signals with hours elapsed
- Color-coded status:
  - **Urgent** (red) - Less than 4 hours, needs immediate attention
  - **Needs Attention** (amber) - Less than 24 hours
  - **Overdue** (red border) - 24+ hours with no response
  - **Responded** (green) - Touchpoint logged after the signal
- Click through to the account to take action

**Win/Loss Patterns**
- Bar chart showing which email signal types (pricing request, objection, urgency, etc.) tend to precede won vs. lost deals
- Helps identify which customer behaviors predict success or risk

**Signal Overview**
- Volume of each intent type detected across the organization
- Confidence scores for AI classifications
- Trend over time

**Recent Feed**
- Live stream of the last 30 processed email signals
- Shows sender, intent type, confidence, and linked account/carrier
- Click through to investigate

### Common Use Cases

- Check the Urgency Tracker daily to ensure no customer urgency goes unanswered
- Review Win/Loss Patterns monthly to coach the team on signal recognition
- Use the Recent Feed to spot emerging opportunities or competitive threats

## 18. Financials

### What It Does

Central hub for revenue and margin data. Shows load-level financial performance across all customers and reps.

### Who Uses It

Admins, Directors, NAMs, Sales, and Sales Directors. Only Admins can upload data.

### What's Displayed

## 24. Next Best Action (NBA) Cards

### What They Are

AI-generated recommendation cards that tell you exactly what to do next for each account. They appear on the Dashboard and on individual Company Detail pages.

### Card Types

| Type | Color | Focus |
|------|-------|-------|
| **Protect** | Red | Retention — account is at risk (load decline, stale contact) |
| **Execute** | Amber | Efficiency — something needs doing (overdue task, lane capacity) |
| **Grow** | Green | Expansion — opportunity to increase business (spot-to-contract, coverage gaps) |
| **Deepen** | Blue | Relationship — build deeper connections (single-thread risk) |

### Common NBA Rules

| Rule | What Triggers It |
|------|-----------------|
| Load Decline | Loads dropped 20%+ vs. 3-month average |
| Single-Thread Risk | Only 1 contact, not touched in 14+ days |
| Stale Account | Revenue-generating account, 21+ days without a touch |
| Overdue Task | Task overdue by 7+ days |
| Spot-to-Contract | 3+ spot loads in 180 days with no active award |
| RFP Coverage Gap | 2+ high-volume RFP sites with no assigned contacts |
| Stalled Award | Award 30+ days old with no recent loads |

### How to Act on NBA Cards

Each card offers specific actions:
- **Log Touch** - Opens the touchpoint dialog
- **Send Email** - Opens email compose
- **Create Task** - Creates a follow-up task
- **Dismiss** - Remove with a reason
- **Snooze** - Hide for 3 days

Cards auto-expire after 14 days if not acted on. New cards supersede old ones for the same account.

## 29. Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Account** | A customer company in the CRM |
| **Award** | Lanes won from an RFP — freight you've been awarded to haul |
| **Bench** | The list of carriers contacted for a specific lane |
| **Callout** | A team-shared post on an account (Win, Risk, or Intel) |
| **Corridor** | A geographic freight pattern (e.g., Upper Midwest, Texas to Southeast) |
| **Coverage Profile** | Assessment of carrier coverage on a lane (Stable, Watch, No History) |
| **DNA** | "Down Not Across" — the company tagline and operating philosophy |
| **Fit Score** | 0-100% measure of how well a carrier matches a specific lane |
| **Greenfield** | An unworked account — no touchpoints, no assigned rep |
| **Home Run** | The highest relationship level with a contact (champion status) |
| **Hot Zone** | A location with 5+ weekly loads — indicates strong network presence |
| **Lane** | A specific origin-to-destination freight route |
| **Lane Score** | 0-100 priority rating for a lane in the work queue |
| **Meaningful Conversation** | A touchpoint that substantively advanced the relationship |
| **Momentum Score** | 0-100 account health score based on activity, relationships, and financials |
| **NBA** | Next Best Action — AI-recommended action for an account |
| **OTRI** | Outbound Tender Rejection Index — freight market tightness indicator |
| **Procurement** | The process of finding and securing carrier capacity for awarded lanes |
| **Recurring Lane** | A freight route that moves consistently (identified from TMS data) |
| **Relationship Base** | Your depth of relationship with a contact (1st, 2nd, 3rd, Home Run) |
| **RFP** | Request for Proposal — a customer's formal request for carrier pricing |
| **Single-Thread Risk** | An account with only one contact — vulnerable if that person leaves |
| **SLA** | Service Level Agreement — the time window for responding to a conversation |
| **SONAR** | FreightWaves market data platform providing real-time freight indices |
| **Spot vs. Contract** | Spot = one-time shipment; Contract = ongoing committed freight |
| **Stale Account** | An active account with no recent touchpoints |
| **TMS** | Transportation Management System — the software that generates load data |
| **Touchpoint** | Any logged interaction with a contact (call, email, text, visit) |
| **VOTRI** | Volume-weighted OTRI — market urgency indicator per lane |
| **Wallet Share** | Your percentage of a customer's total freight spend |
