You are designing and implementing a clean relationship-to-freight attribution model inside Freight DNA.

Context:
Freight DNA tracks:
- contacts with relationship base (1st, 2nd, 3rd, Home Run)
- lane attributions per contact (origin → destination)
- inbound financial load data (loads, margin, contract %, spot %)

Goal:
Make the Relationship Freight Reporting accurate and easy to use:
- when freight is assigned to a contact (directly or via lane attribution),
  loads and margin should roll up to that contact’s relationship base,
  so we can see freight performance by 1st/2nd/3rd/Home Run and “Unworked”.

Current pain:
The process for assigning freight to contacts/relationship bases feels clunky and the reporting does not always reflect reality (e.g., freight shipped but relationship metrics not updating correctly or on time).

Your job:
1) Audit the current relationship freight pipeline:
   - how freight records are linked to companies and contacts
   - how lane attributions are stored and resolved
   - how loads/margin are currently rolled up to relationship levels
   - how “Unworked Accounts” is calculated
2) Identify accuracy and UX issues:
   - freight with no mapped contact or base
   - multiple contacts tied to the same freight/lane
   - changes in relationship base over time
   - refresh / recalculation timing
3) Propose a clear attribution model for this product, such as:
   - one primary contact per account gets credit for freight on assigned lanes, OR
   - split attribution when multiple contacts own lanes, OR
   - snapshot relationship base at time of load, etc.
   Choose and justify the model that best fits Freight DNA’s goals.
4) Recommend UX changes to make assignment and reporting less clunky:
   - how reps assign lanes to contacts
   - how they see the impact in Relationship Freight Performance and Coverage
   - how “Unworked” is defined and displayed.

Output format:
A. Current data model and flow
B. Accuracy and usability problems
C. Recommended attribution model (with examples)
D. Required schema/logic changes
E. Recommended UX changes to Relationship Freight reporting
F. Implementation plan (phased, lowest-risk first)
Then wait for approval before implementing.
