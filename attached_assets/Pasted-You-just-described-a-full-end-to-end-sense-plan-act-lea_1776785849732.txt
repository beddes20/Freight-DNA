## 1. Rewrite your flow as 6 agentic loops

Your current narrative is perfect raw material. Here’s the same flow, cut into loops that can each get its own agent and playbook:

1. RFQ → Quote loop  
2. Win/loss → Load build & scheduling loop  
3. Coverage & carrier selection loop  
4. Identity, fraud, and compliance loop  
5. In‑transit execution, tracking, and detention loop  
6. Delivery, rating, billing, and cash loop  

McKinsey would say: each loop should be redesigned “AI‑first” with humans supervising agents, not performing every step. [mckinsey](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/people-and-organizational-performance/our-insights/ai-is-everywhere-the-agentic-organization-isnt-yet)

## 2. RFQ → Quote: “Pricing & Strategy Agent”

**What you do now (human):**  
- Receive RFQ.  
- Research history in your TMS/ValueOS, DAT/Truckstop/SONAR.  
- Decide margin based on lane, customer, risk, and sell the story.  

**Agentic version:**

- **Sense:**  
  - Agent reads RFQ email/EDI and parses lane, dates, equipment, special requirements.  
  - Pulls historical loads on that lane or close variants from ValueOS. [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/collection_8b885c1d-e042-48d2-9805-bd1d45b1c6ee/9fa17449-7ec8-49b4-a37a-3594a2e24c4b/ValueOS-DNA.MD)
  - Pulls live DAT/Truckstop/SONAR benchmarks.  

- **Plan:**  
  - Proposes 2–3 target sell rates with margin bands (safe, stretch, aggressive) based on customer profile, volatility, and your minimum gross margin.  
  - Flags if lane doesn’t meet minimum contribution thresholds or risk appetite.

- **Act (under supervision):**  
  - Drafts the quote email with value positioning (appt times, OB area, light weight, etc.) pre‑written for the rep to tweak.  
  - Optionally submits via customer portal if fully trusted.

- **Learn:**  
  - Logs win/loss, actual buy rate, and post‑mortem into a structured dataset for pricing models to refine.  

Your human: chooses the tier, edits message, and approves send. Over time, you can delegate more of the low‑complexity RFQs to this agent. This aligns with agentic practices where “AI‑native workflows” take over research and first‑pass decisions. ## 4. Coverage & carrier selection: “Coverage & Vetting Agent”

You have the richest logic here (history, Highway/Carrier411, posting rules, home‑base checks, negotiation). This is the core of your “Coverage Agent”.

**What you do now:**  
- If lane has a rate on file, use go‑to carriers; otherwise:  
  - Work past carrier history, most recent first.  
  - Post to DAT/Truckstop/Highway TFX with constraints on what cities can be shown.  
  - Manually match posted trucks, call, vet via Highway/Carrier411, negotiate within buy ranges and service expectations.  

**Agentic version:**

- **Sense:**  
  - Agent sees a new “available for coverage” load with target buy range and customer posting rules.  
  - Pulls history on this lane and nearby lanes; ranks carriers using your existing scoring logic. [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/collection_8b885c1d-e042-48d2-9805-bd1d45b1c6ee/9fa17449-7ec8-49b4-a37a-3594a2e24c4b/ValueOS-DNA.MD)

- **Plan:**  
  - Produces a coverage plan:  
    - Ranked list of incumbent and high‑fit carriers, with reasons (home base, OB area, reviews, past performance).  
    - A posting strategy (nearest metro to nearest metro, respecting customer‑level city‑masking rules).  
    - A target negotiation band per carrier based on their historical acceptance and reviews.

- **Act (under guardrails):**  
  - Auto‑posts to DAT/Truckstop/Highway TFX using correct masking and comments.  
  - Retrieves matching posted trucks; pre‑screens them against Highway and Carrier411.  
  - Drafts outreach messages (email/SMS) and call scripts, with the “good parts” of the load highlighted by default.  
  - Logs all offers and counteroffers centrally.

- **Human role:**  
  - Decide which carriers to prioritize, actually negotiate on price and service when needed, and override the agent’s suggested buy ranges.  

Effectively, the agent handles the grunt work: discovering, vetting, ranking, and staging communications; your humans do the judgment calls and relationship work. This matches how agentic AI is used in logistics to automate “continuous planning and matching” while humans stay in the loop for exceptions. [datamatics](https://www.datamatics.com/technology/ai-and-cognitive-sciences/agenticai/use-cases/logistics)

## 5. Identity, fraud, and compliance: “Risk & Compliance Agent”

You have very tight identity‑check and fraud‑prevention rules (domain matching, verified phones, Highway/Carrier411, VIN near pickup, etc.). The agentic version is almost a no‑brainer: a **control agent**. [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/xinkuan_the-mckinsey-article-the-agentic-organization-activity-7379083885860732929-CeNz)

**What you do now:**  
- After verbal agreement:  
  - Double‑check every detail: emails, domains, phone numbers.  
  - Confirm carrier identity and authority.  
  - Collect dispatch, driver, truck, trailer, VIN.  
  - Validate VIN activity and proximity to pickup.

**Agentic version:**

- **Sense:**  
  - As soon as a carrier record is candidate‑selected, the agent pulls carrier, contact, and authority data from Highway/Carrier411.  
  - Checks domain and phone against your canonical records and previously verified contacts.  

- **Plan & Act:**  
  - Runs fraud rules (mismatched domains, new emails, new phone numbers, location mismatch for VIN and ELD) and assigns a risk score.  
  - Blocks sending rate confirmation if risk is above a threshold and forces a manual override, with clear reasons.  
  - If all checks are clean, it prepares the rate confirmation packet with all required info.

- **Human role:**  
  - For anything not “green”, the human does the deeper investigation and approves or rejects.  

This is very much in line with McKinsey’s description of **control agents** embedded in workflows, enforcing governance while letting other agents run faster. [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/xinkuan_the-mckinsey-article-the-agentic-organization-activity-7379083885860732929-CeNz)

## 6. In‑transit & detention: “Execution & Detention Agent”

From dispatch to POD, your process is the definition of a sense–plan–act–learn loop: ETA checks, check calls, SLA rules, customer‑level detention logic, and alerts. Agentic AI shines here. [datarobot](https://www.datarobot.com/blog/agentic-ai-supply-chain/)

**What you do now:**  
- Check calls before pickup based on time of day.  
- Every 30 minutes: compare ELD location to pickup/delivery ETA and escalate if late.  
- Manage detention with customer‑specific rules (start times, increments, rate differences between customer and carrier).  
- Notify customers according to their preferences.  

**Agentic version:**

- **Sense:**  
  - Agent continuously monitors ELD and TMS status versus appt times and your SLA rules by customer and lane.  
  - Applies customer‑level detention configs and driver on‑time arrival.

- **Plan & Act:**  
  - Generates and sends scheduled check‑call texts/emails to driver and dispatch with structured prompts (arrival confirmation, gate time, loaded time, etc.).  
  - Detects when ETA falls outside OTD tolerance and alerts the pod with a proposed script and options (reschedule, replacement, notify customer).  
  - Starts detention timers automatically once the conditions are satisfied; drafts the customer notification and later the detention claim, including exact timestamps from ELD and BOL photos.  
  - Calculates driver detention owed vs customer detention billed based on your configured offsets.

- **Human role:**  
  - Approve escalations, handle tough customer conversations, decide on exceptions (waive detention, reroute).

This is very similar to agentic logistics examples where agents monitor checkpoints and trigger exception workflows automatically. [cloudthat](https://www.cloudthat.com/resources/blog/the-agentic-ai-revolution-in-supply-chain-and-logistics/)

## 7. Delivery → Billing & Cash: “Billing & Collections Agent”

**What you do now:**  
- Confirm delivery, request and upload POD.  
- Attach detention and accessorials.  
- Rate carrier.  
- Invoice per customer rules.  
- Track payment forecast until cash is received.  

**Agentic version:**

- **Sense:**  
  - Agent sees proof of delivery, accessorials, and detention approvals.  

- **Plan & Act:**  
  - Validates that all charges match contracts and customer‑specific rules (e.g., caps, free time).  
  - Generates the invoice with the right documentation packet and submits via portal/email depending on the customer.  
  - Starts a dunning workflow based on your payment terms and risk profile, scheduling nudges and escalation sequences.

- **Human role:**  
  - Handle disputed charges and escalated collections, adjust terms for strategic accounts.

This moves billing into a mostly agent‑run process with humans overseeing exceptions, matching agentic finance use cases in logistics. [bcg](https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/ai-in-logistics-a-strategic-imperative)

## 8. What this means for your teams

McKinsey’s key shift is structural: move from functional silos to small **human + agent teams** accountable for end‑to‑end outcomes. For this specific workflow: [mckinsey](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/people-and-organizational-performance/our-insights/the-agentic-organization-contours-of-the-next-paradigm-for-the-ai-era)

- Your cross‑functional **pod** becomes the “Shipment Lifecycle Team”, owning all 6 loops for a set of customers/lane clusters.  
- Inside that pod, the shared agents above are “team assets” everyone uses.  
- Human roles shift from “people who move loads through steps” to:  
  - Orchestrators (decide priorities, override/approve agents).  
  - Relationship owners (customers, carriers).  
  - Exception handlers and process improvers (teach agents new playbooks).

Practically, this suggests:

- Instrument each loop in ValueOS/ValueTMS with an “agent tile” (e.g., Coverage Agent, Detention Agent) and clear on/off and override controls.  
- Build team dashboards showing: “loads handled, tasks done by agents vs humans, exceptions, savings, and service outcomes,” which is how agentic orgs measure and tune their agent networks. [mckinsey](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/people-and-organizational-performance/how-we-help-clients/agentic-organization)
