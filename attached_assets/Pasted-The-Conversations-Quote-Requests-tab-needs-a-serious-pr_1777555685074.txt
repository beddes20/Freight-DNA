The Conversations → Quote Requests tab needs a serious product and data-model pass. I want you to investigate it as a core workflow surface, not just an inbox screen.

My vision for this tab

This tab should become the system of record for inbound quote-request email workflow:

every quote-request email we receive,

who owns it,

whether we responded,

how we responded,

whether AI can learn from that response behavior,

and how it connects to Customer Quotes, analytics, and eventual won/lost/no-response outcomes.

Right now it feels siloed and not aligned with that vision.

Specific problems visible today

The UI is hard to scan and digest.

Freshness / “Updated” values appear misleading.

Example: in the list, thread 157168 / 620787 shows Updated 22h ago,

but when opened, the visible email content is from 4/10 (roughly 20 days ago).

I need to know exactly what “Updated” currently means, why it diverges from actual thread activity, and what it should mean in this workflow.

The tab does not feel deeply connected enough to quote workflow, response tracking, or downstream analytics.

What I want you to investigate first

Before coding, do a full investigation of:

A. Data model / freshness semantics

What data source drives the Quote Requests list?

What field currently powers the list’s “Updated” timestamp?

latest inbound/outbound email event,

thread summary regeneration,

assignment/status mutation,

quote-request classification time,

or some other internal update?

B. Workflow linkage

For each quote-request thread, determine:

how it links (or fails to link) to Customer Quotes / quote_opportunities,

how response state is derived,

how “waiting on us / waiting on them / resolved” is determined,

how win/loss/no-response should eventually attach to this view,

and what fields/events are missing to support that cleanly.

C. UX / information architecture

Propose how the Quote Requests list and detail pane should be redesigned so a rep/admin can quickly answer:

what this is,

who owns it,

what the customer wants,

what we last did,

and what the current disposition is.
