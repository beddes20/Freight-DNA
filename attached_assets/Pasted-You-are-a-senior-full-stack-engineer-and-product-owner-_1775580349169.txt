You are a senior full-stack engineer and product owner for Freight DNA.

Context:
Freight DNA is a freight-broker-specific CRM and sales intelligence platform with:
- CRM core (companies, contacts, relationship bases 1st–Home Run)
- touchpoint logging
- NBA engine and Account Growth/Health Scores
- dashboard portlets (Account Growth, Priority Actions Today, etc.)
- Pre-Call Planner
- LM check-ins, daily digests, etc.

Goal:
Tighten the platform so that core workflows are accurate, consistent, and trustworthy.
We are seeing issues where data appears in one place but not in another
(e.g., a call touchpoint logged for ACH Foods shows in Today’s Touchpoints, but the Account Growth portlet says “No touchpoints on record”).

Your job:
Perform a targeted audit of core data flows and fix inconsistencies.

Please:
1) Map end-to-end flows for:
   - touchpoint → company/contact → health/growth scores
   - touchpoint → “Today’s Touchpoints”
   - touchpoint → NBA engine
   - touchpoint → Account Growth & Priority Actions portlets
2) Identify all places where:
   - data is present but not reflected in scores/portlets, or
   - cached/derived values get stale after updates.
3) For each inconsistency, identify whether the root cause is:
   - data linkage (wrong company/contact IDs)
   - missing or incorrect cache invalidation
   - outdated rollup logic
   - UI using different logic than the core engine.
4) Propose the minimal, permanent fixes that bring all surfaces into alignment.
   - Scores and portlets should always reflect current touchpoint and relationship data within an acceptable delay.
   - “No touchpoints on record” should only appear when there truly are none.
5) Prioritize fixes for:
   - Account Growth portlet
   - Priority Actions / NBA portlet
   - company detail Account Growth & Health sections.

Output format:
A. Data flows and where each UI surface reads from
B. List of inconsistencies found (with specific examples)
C. Root cause analysis for each inconsistency
D. Proposed fixes (by file/function, in priority order)
E. Implementation plan for the top 3–5 fixes
Then stop and wait for approval before changing code.
