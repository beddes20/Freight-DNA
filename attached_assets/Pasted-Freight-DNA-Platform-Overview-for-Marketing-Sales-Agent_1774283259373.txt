Freight DNA — Platform Overview for Marketing/Sales Agent
What It Is
Freight DNA is a purpose-built CRM and sales intelligence platform designed exclusively for transportation brokerage sales teams. It is not a general-purpose CRM — every feature is tailored to the workflows, language, and data challenges unique to freight brokerage. The platform runs in a web browser and is accessible to the entire sales organization simultaneously, with each user seeing only the data and tools relevant to their role.
Who It's For
The platform supports a multi-tier sales organization:
Admins (e.g., VP of Sales) — full visibility across the entire organization, all accounts, all reps, all data
Directors — oversee multiple NAMs and their teams
National Account Managers (NAMs) — manage a team of Account Managers, set goals, run 1:1 meetings, and handle large strategic accounts
Account Managers (AMs) — own day-to-day customer relationships, log activity, manage contacts and org charts
Sales / Sales Directors — additional sales roles with appropriate scoping
Logistics Managers / Coordinators — operational staff who can be paired into 1:1 discussions and tracked
All data is filtered by role automatically. An AM only sees their own accounts. A NAM sees their team's accounts. An admin sees everything.
Core Features
1. Company & Contact Management (The CRM Foundation)
Users manage a list of customer accounts (companies). Each company has a detailed profile that includes:
Basic info: company name, industry, address
Account ownership: which AM owns it
A full contact list with titles, phone numbers, emails, and reporting structures
Organizational chart — a visual, hierarchical display of who reports to whom inside the customer's organization. This is the signature feature that gives the platform its name.
Transportation-specific intelligence fields: how the customer tenders freight (TMS portal, email, EDI), their spot freight process, dispatch/logistics email, account quirks, and standard process notes
Portal credentials: customer portal URL, login credentials, and a "Financial Name" alias used to match the account to financial data uploads
2. Organizational Chart Visualization
Each customer account has a built-in org chart builder. Reps add contacts and define reporting relationships (who reports to whom). The platform renders a live, hierarchical org chart so reps can instantly see the power structure within a customer account — who the decision-makers are, who the day-to-day contacts are, and where coverage gaps exist. Contacts on the org chart show color-coded "recency dots" indicating how recently a rep has been in touch with that person.
3. Touchpoints (Activity Tracking)
Reps log every customer interaction — calls, emails, texts, and site visits — directly on contact records. Each touchpoint can include a note. The platform then:
Shows a "last touch" recency badge on every contact (green = recent, amber = getting cold, red = overdue at 30+ days)
Displays a weekly touch count per contact
Surfaces a "Contacts Needing Attention" alert on the dashboard for any contact untouched for 30+ days
Tracks a touchpoint activity feed per account
Supports a "Quick Log Touch" button on both the customer list and company detail pages — one click to log an interaction without navigating away
4. RFP & Award Management
This is the platform's most advanced feature set. When a customer issues a Request for Proposal (RFP) for their freight network, reps upload the RFP as an Excel file and the platform automatically:
Parses every lane (origin city/state → destination city/state, equipment type, annual volume, weekly volume)
Extracts the top high-volume lanes for focused attention
Identifies which states the freight originates from and delivers to
Tracks bid status (pending, won, lost, in progress)
Stores the RFP value, due date, and notes
Triggers deadline alerts on the dashboard when an RFP is due within 14 days
Awards track which lanes were actually won from each RFP, creating a historical record of wins and losses.
5. Lane Research & Assignment
From each RFP's high-volume lanes, reps can assign ownership — specifying who is responsible for researching and winning each lane. This turns RFP data into actionable work items distributed across the team.
6. Analytical Features (Data Intelligence)
The platform includes several analytical tools that turn raw RFP and delivery data into sales insights:
Facility Coverage Gap Analysis — compares RFP lane destinations against existing contact coverage to identify facilities that have no assigned planner or contact. Surfaces these as tasks for the team.
Lane Pattern Analysis — identifies top freight corridors (state-to-state volume), shipping hubs (cities generating the most outbound freight), and receiving hubs (most common delivery destinations)
Historical Data Analysis — when financial/account summary data is uploaded (via Excel or OneDrive sync), the platform maps historical delivery destinations, identifies "hot zones" (high-frequency delivery areas), and surfaces historical lane corridors
Top Opportunities Engine — cross-references a customer's RFP lane origins against their historical delivery destinations to find where the company's existing freight network overlaps with the customer's needs — identifying the highest-probability lanes to win
Proximity Matches — identifies delivery zones within a 75-mile radius of customer RFP pickup origins, surfacing nearby opportunities the team might have missed
Lane Matching Portlet — overlaps the company's historical freight network with customer RFP lanes to identify backhaul opportunities and delivery lane matches
7. Financial Data Integration (OneDrive Sync)
The platform connects to a Microsoft OneDrive shared folder to automatically fetch monthly financial/account-summary Excel files. This sync:
Pulls rep-level financial data (load counts, margin, revenue by account)
Matches accounts using the company's "Financial Name" alias
Populates the Team Performance dashboard with real financial metrics
Can be triggered manually or runs automatically on the first business day of each month
Shows an admin alert banner if the sync fails
8. Team Performance Dashboard
Admins and NAMs have a dedicated team performance view showing each rep's financial metrics side by side — loads booked, margin generated, revenue. Data is sourced from the monthly financial sync and matched to user records via Financial Rep IDs. This gives leadership a real-time snapshot of team performance without building spreadsheets.
9. Goals System
NAMs set monthly or custom-period goals for each of their AMs across five metric types:
New Contacts Added (auto-tracked from contact creation)
Touchpoints (auto-tracked from logged interactions)
Load Count (manually updated)
Margin (manually updated)
Custom (user-defined metric with a custom label)
Each goal shows a progress bar, percentage complete, and inline comments where NAMs and AMs can discuss progress. A "Goals Leaderboard" shows rankings across the team. On the 15th of each month, if any AM is under 50% on a goal, a "Goals Need Attention" alert appears on the dashboard for the NAM. If an NAM hasn't set goals for their AMs by month-end, a "Monthly Goals Not Set" alert appears.
10. 1:1 Discussions (Meeting Management)
Every NAM↔AM pairing has a dedicated, persistent 1:1 discussion thread. Both parties can:
Add topics at any time with tags: Action Item, Question, FYI, Follow-up, Shoutout, Let's Work On
Mark topics as discussed (with an "Undo" button to revert if needed)
Post threaded replies under any topic
Close a session, which archives discussed topics and carries pending ones forward to the next session
The platform shows a badge on the dashboard with the count of pending undiscussed topics, keeping 1:1 agendas visible at all times.
Key integration with the Task system: Any topic tagged as "Action Item" in a 1:1 session automatically appears in the "My Tasks" portlet on the dashboard under a "1:1 Action Items" section — so action items from meetings are never lost.
11. Task Management
A lightweight task system lets anyone on the team create and assign tasks with:
Title, notes, due date
Assignee and assigner
Optional company link
Status cycling: Open → In Progress → Completed
Color-coded due date badges (red = overdue, amber = due today, yellow = due soon)
Comment threads per task
File attachments
Tasks can be created in several ways: manually, from a contact card ("+" button on "Contacts Needing Attention"), from facility coverage gap analysis, or from lane research assignment. Tasks assigned to the current user appear in their "My Tasks" portlet on the dashboard.
12. Callouts / Trends Feed
A company-wide social feed where all users post updates, trends, wins, and ideas. Posts support:
Categories: Growth Win, Trend, Callout, Idea, Shoutout, Let's Work On
Optional company tag (links the post to a specific account)
Threaded replies
Emoji reactions
@mentions of teammates
File and image attachments (including paste-to-upload for screenshots)
The dashboard shows recent posts across the team. Each company's profile page shows only posts tagged to that specific account.
13. Leadership Callouts (Internal Comms)
A private, leadership-only communication channel visible only to admins and directors. Allows leadership to send targeted messages to specific people (Jordan, Danny, Sam, or any combination) with full file/image attachment support. Completely separate from the public feed — invisible to AMs and NAMs.
14. PTO Passoff
When a rep goes on PTO, they create a formal passoff record specifying:
Date range
Who is covering
Emergency contact info
Per-account checklist cards capturing: priority level (High/Medium/Low), spot freight handler, key customer contact, open items, process notes, and active RFPs/bids
The covering rep gets a dedicated "I'm Covering" tab and can acknowledge each account. When the rep returns, they close the passoff with a "Close & Return Accounts" button.
15. Customer Scorecard
Each company profile has a Scorecard section where reps can upload and download scorecard documents (PDFs, Word, Excel, images) directly on the account page. Useful for storing quarterly business review documents, customer scorecards, or performance summaries.
16. Account Intelligence
Each company profile has a structured "Account Intelligence" section with free-text fields covering:
Tendering Process (how the customer sends freight: TMS, email, EDI, etc.)
Spot Process (how they handle spot freight)
D/L Email (dispatch/logistics contact email)
Account Quirks (sensitivities, unusual requirements, things to know)
Process Notes (standard operating procedures for the account)
This knowledge base ensures that institutional knowledge stays with the account — not just in someone's head.
17. Dashboard (Command Center)
The dashboard is the daily starting point for every user. It surfaces:
KPI stat cards (total companies, contacts, touchpoints this month, active RFPs)
"Contacts Needing Attention" — contacts untouched 30+ days with a quick "+" to create a follow-up task
"My Tasks" — open tasks assigned to the user, plus 1:1 Action Items
RFP Deadlines Approaching — red alert for RFPs due within 14 days
Goals Need Attention — amber alert mid-month for goals under 50%
Monthly Goals Not Set — alert for NAMs who haven't set AM goals
1:1 Discussion portlet with pending topic count badge
Callouts/Trends feed
Goals leaderboard
18. Global Search
A live, debounced global search bar that searches across all companies and users simultaneously as the user types — no page navigation required.
19. Notifications
A notification bell in the header shows real-time alerts for:
New tasks assigned to you
Task comments
New contacts added to your accounts
New feed posts and replies
Goal updates
1:1 topic additions and session closures
New company accounts added
RFP deadline reminders
20. File Attachments
Throughout the platform, users can attach files (PDFs, Word docs, Excel/CSV, images) to:
Feed posts and replies
1:1 discussion topics
Touchpoints
Tasks
Customer scorecards
Leadership callouts
Images can be pasted directly from the clipboard (screenshot paste) with a lightbox preview before and after posting. Files are stored securely and downloadable by authorized users.
21. User Management & Account Transfers
Admins and NAMs can:
Create, edit, and deactivate user accounts
Assign roles and set manager hierarchy
Set Financial Rep IDs (used for financial data matching)
Transfer company accounts from one AM to another (useful for territory changes or rep turnover)
Key Differentiators vs. Generic CRMs
Built for freight, not adapted for it — fields like equipment type, tender style, lane data, spot process, and financial alias are first-class concepts, not custom fields bolted on
RFP intelligence engine — no other CRM automatically parses freight RFP Excel files and surfaces lane opportunities, gap analysis, and backhaul matches
Org chart as a core feature — not a bolt-on; visualizing power structures within customer accounts is central to the sales strategy
Financial data integration — directly connects to the company's own financial data via OneDrive to populate rep performance metrics automatically
Role hierarchy that mirrors transportation sales orgs — Admin → Director → NAM → AM → Logistics Manager/Coordinator is built into every data access decision
Touchpoint recency as a visual signal — the org chart and contact cards show at a glance which relationships are going cold, prompting proactive outreach
Action items that don't disappear — 1:1 action items surface on the dashboard task list so they're never buried in meeting notes
PTO continuity — the passoff system ensures account coverage doesn't break down when reps are out
