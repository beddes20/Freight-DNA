You are a senior product engineer and customer success strategist working on Freight-DNA.

Context:
Freight-DNA is a freight brokerage CRM/platform built to help teams grow deeper with existing customers, improve rep adoption, and make account management more strategic. The product should feel freight-native, practical, and easy to act on.

Important context:
The Account Growth Score feature is already live.
- Accounts now have a growth score and score band
- The score appears on the dashboard, customers page, and company detail page
- The score is rules-based and designed to surface expansion potential and risk

Feature goal:
Design a "Next Best Action" engine that uses the existing Account Growth Score plus account signals to recommend the single most useful next step for each account.

What the feature should help users answer:
- What should I do next for this customer?
- Why is that the best action right now?
- Is this account at risk, stable, or ready to expand?
- What action should a rep, manager, or leader take?
- How urgent is it?

Use freight-specific actions such as:
- schedule a QBR
- re-engage a dormant contact
- follow up on a service issue
- escalate a save motion
- introduce a new service or mode
- explore additional lanes / locations
- add another stakeholder
- review margin erosion
- create or update an account plan
- reach out after inactivity

Requirements:
1. Start by designing the action framework before writing code.
2. Make the MVP rules-based and transparent.
3. Use the existing Account Growth Score as one of the decision inputs, not the only one.
4. Each recommended action should include:
   - action name
   - why it was triggered
   - urgency
   - owner
   - expected outcome
5. Show how the recommended action changes based on:
   - score band
   - recent activity
   - service/risk signals
   - relationship depth
   - growth opportunity indicators
6. Recommend where this should appear in the UI:
   - dashboard
   - company detail page
   - task list / alerts
   - leader view
7. Include fallback behavior when there is no clear action.
8. Keep the UI simple and decisive — one best action, not a cluttered list.
9. Do not code yet. First provide the design and implementation plan.

Please return:
A. Action framework
B. Trigger rules
C. Recommended action catalog
D. UI placement recommendations
E. MVP vs later version
F. Data needed from the app
G. Risks / edge cases
H. Step-by-step implementation plan
Then stop and wait for approval before coding.
