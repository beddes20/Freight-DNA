I want to tighten our carrier ranking logic so we suggest the most logical carriers for the lane, not just “anyone who has ever run freight for this customer.”

The analysis you gave me is correct:

We have a Lane Fit score (history + geography + equipment).

We have a Performance Scorecard (Tier A/B/C).

We have a heavier LWQ ranker that adds outreach/intel/recency/customer history.

We have a Recommendation Engine that blends fit and performance for Available Loads.

The problem: in some cases the system leans too hard on customer-specific history, so carriers who have run for this customer on other lanes get suggested even when their lane fit is weak, and can outrank carriers with strong lane history.

I want to re‑bias the logic so:

Lane + location history and carrier profile are the primary gate and weight.

Customer history is a secondary boost / tiebreaker, not a primary inclusion rule.

Carriers with strong lane history and good profile can outrank “customer-only” carriers.

Focus changes on:

server/carrierRankingService.ts (rankCarriersForLane — LWQ, AF cockpit, procurement outreach).

server/carrierRecommendationEngine.ts (recommendCarriersForLoad — Available Loads, AI tools).

carrierLaneFitService.ts and carrierScorecardService.ts (fit + profile core).

Goal

After this change, when we suggest carriers:

Lane fit + carrier profile are the core of the score:

exact lane history (city pair + equipment, runs),

nearby lane history (≤75 miles),

region/state‑pair history,

equipment match,

recency (decay),

realized performance on those lanes (margin, on‑time),

accepted intel/profile (lane prefs, equipment capability, capacity signals, HQ proximity).

Customer history is an additive bonus, only applied after lane fit is established.

A carrier with strong lane history and good profile but no customer history can outrank a weak‑fit carrier with lots of customer history on other lanes.

Carriers are never suggested near the top just because they’ve run for this customer before if they are a poor lane match.

Required logic changes

Minimum lane-fit threshold for serious suggestions

Introduce a minimum lane‑fit score (e.g. 40–50, configurable) below which a carrier cannot appear in the top N suggestions, regardless of customer history, unless:

there are effectively no other candidates. In that thin-lane case, they can appear as fallbacks with a clear weak‑fit reason.

Lane-first blend in both engines

In rankCarriersForLane and recommendCarriersForLoad, adjust the final score formula so:

lane fit + profile (as defined above) contribute the majority of the score (target ≥ 60–70%).

performance/tier and customer history refine ordering, not core inclusion.

In the heavy LWQ ranker:

apply geography/equipment/recency floors and decay before customer history boosts.

Customer history as secondary boost

Keep existing customer‑history boosts, but:

only apply them after the lane‑fit floor is satisfied, and

reduce their relative size so they cannot outweigh strong lane fit.

A strong lane‑fit carrier with no customer history must be able to outrank a mediocre‑fit carrier with strong customer history.

Explicit handling of “customer-only” carriers

If a carrier has customer history but no meaningful lane evidence (e.g., only region‑level/general customer history, weak lane fit below the threshold):

either exclude them from the top N suggestions, or

show them only as fallbacks, clearly flagged as:

“Customer history only on other lanes (weak lane fit)”.

They should not appear above carriers with strong lane history and good profile fit.

Geography priority

Reconfirm the lane‑fit tiers (carrierLaneFitService.ts / ranker constants):

exact lane > nearby (≤75 mi both ends) > state‑pair/region > no evidence.

Ensure exact/nearby lane + correct equipment always beats pure customer history on unrelated lanes.
