Good news — the system is actually quite well built out for this. Here's what I found:

Yes, the carrier intelligence pipeline is already wired up end-to-end. Here's how it works:

The Full Loop
Outreach goes out via LWQ → carrier gets an email about a specific lane

Carrier replies → Microsoft Graph webhook catches the inbound email, matches it to the original outreach thread, and logs it

AI reads the reply → Every 10 minutes, the Email Intelligence scheduler picks up unprocessed replies and uses GPT-4o-mini to extract structured signals:

Lanes they run (origin/destination/equipment)
Capacity available or unavailable
Rate pushback or pricing info
Soft or hard commitments
Carrier Hub gets updated → Those extracted signals become "Intel Suggestions" that appear in the Carrier Hub for your team to review. When accepted, they update the carrier's profile — regions served, equipment types, and claimed lanes.

Future LWQ recommendations improve → The next time a lane needs carrier outreach, the ranking engine uses all of this:

Carriers who previously showed interest in that exact lane get a +35 point boost
Carriers whose profile regions match the lane get a +15 point boost
Carriers with accepted lane preferences get an +8 boost
Carriers marked available from past outreach get a +12 boost
Carriers flagged as unavailable get suppressed (-6 penalty)
Reliability Score — Each carrier builds a 0–100 reliability score based on reply rate (40%), hard commitment rate (30%), and positive outcome rate (30%). This score is visible in the Carrier Hub.

So to directly answer your question:
The emails are being read intelligently and the data does flow back into recommendations. The system learns which carriers run which lanes, tracks their responsiveness, and surfaces the best carriers for future work.

The one thing to note: the intel suggestions from email replies do require a human to "Accept" them in the Carrier Hub before they permanently update the carrier profile. This is by design — it keeps a human in the loop so bad AI extractions don't pollute carrier data. Would you want that to be fully automatic instead, or does the review step make sense for your team?
