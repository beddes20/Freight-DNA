Rebuild Customer Quotes as the PRIMARY freight quoting cockpit for reps.

This is not just a quote-entry tab.
This screen must be the main operating surface where a rep can:
- intake a customer quote request,
- review and parse quote-related emails,
- compare against similar historical quotes,
- compare against similar freight we already have,
- compare against what we have paid carriers on similar lanes,
- decide what to quote,
- record what we quoted,
- track whether we won or lost,
- and learn why.

PRODUCT VISION
Customer Quotes should function like a freight pricing + quote intelligence engine.
A rep should be able to open this page and answer:
1) What is the customer asking for?
2) What does the email thread actually say?
3) What have we quoted on similar lanes before?
4) What have we paid on similar freight before?
5) What is the likely market / internal range?
6) What should we quote now?
7) Did we win or lose?
8) Why did we win or lose?
9) What can we learn for the next quote?

I want the MAIN screen to reflect that full workflow again.
If parts of that prior structure were removed, restore the best version of it and improve it — do not reduce this to a simple form.

BUILD THE SCREEN AROUND THESE 6 CORE PANELS

1. Quote Intake / Summary panel
Purpose: establish the quote request clearly.
Show:
- customer
- contact
- quote request status
- origin / destination / stops
- equipment
- pickup / delivery timing
- weight / commodity if available
- requested price / target if present
- source of request (email, manual entry, other)
- assigned rep / team
- confidence score that the request data is complete
Actions:
- create quote
- update quote
- mark needs clarification
- mark no quote
- assign owner

2. Email Intelligence panel
Purpose: turn raw email traffic into structured quote intelligence.
Show:
- related email thread / messages
- extracted fields from emails (origin, destination, equipment, date, customer target, special instructions, etc.)
- confidence and extraction warnings
- highlighted pricing signals from email text
- customer urgency / deadline cues
- missing fields detected from the thread
Actions:
- accept extracted fields into quote
- override extracted values
- jump to original thread
- flag parsing issue
- compare current customer ask vs extracted ask
Important:
This is not just an email viewer. It must actively analyze and structure the quote information from email content.

3. Similar Quotes / Historical Intelligence panel
Purpose: show what we quoted before on similar freight.
Show:
- similar prior quotes by lane / lane-cluster / region / customer / equipment / timing
- what we quoted
- whether we won or lost
- margin / spread if available
- recency
- confidence / relevance score
- explanation of similarity (same lane, same customer, same region pair, same equipment, etc.)
Actions:
- open prior quote
- pin a comp
- filter similarity basis
- sort by recency / similarity / outcome

4. Similar Freight / Internal Cost panel
Purpose: compare against actual freight and what the company has paid on comparable loads.
Show:
- similar booked freight
- customer / lane / equipment / date
- what we paid the carrier
- what we charged if available
- margin if available
- booked / covered / delivered status
- recency
- confidence / similarity reason
Actions:
- toggle between quote comps and actual freight comps
- show nearest lane matches
- show exact lane matches only
Important:
This is where reps understand “what we are paying on similar lanes as a company.”

5. Pricing Decision panel
Purpose: help the rep decide the quote.
Show:
- recommended quote range
- floor / target / stretch numbers
- explanation of how the range was formed:
  - historical quotes
  - internal freight cost comps
  - email signals
  - customer history
- editable quoted price
- editable reasoning notes
- customer target vs our recommended quote
- win probability / risk indicator if possible
Actions:
- save quote
- send quote
- draft email response
- mark quoted verbally / by email
Important:
Do not make this a black box. Reps need to see the WHY behind the recommendation.

6. Outcome / Postmortem panel
Purpose: close the loop and build institutional knowledge.
Show:
- current outcome: open / quoted / won / lost / no quote / expired
- final quoted price
- winning price if known
- loss reason / win reason
- competitor / carrier info if known
- turnaround time
- lessons / notes
Actions:
- mark won
- mark lost
- mark no quote
- add loss reason
- add follow-up notes
- feed the result back into future quote intelligence
