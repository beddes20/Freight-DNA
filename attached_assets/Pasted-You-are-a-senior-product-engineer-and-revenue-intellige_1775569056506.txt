You are a senior product engineer and revenue intelligence designer working on Freight-DNA.

Context:
Freight-DNA is a freight brokerage CRM/platform designed to help teams grow deeper with existing customers, improve rep adoption, and make account management more strategic. The product should feel freight-native, practical, and easy for reps to trust.

Feature goal:
Design an "Account Growth Score" feature that ranks each customer account by expansion potential and risk.

What the score should help users answer:
- Which accounts deserve attention first?
- Which customers are most likely to expand?
- Which accounts are at risk of slipping?
- Why is this account ranked here?
- What should a rep do next?

Use freight-specific signals such as:
- volume trend
- margin trend
- lane breadth / diversity
- touchpoint frequency and quality
- relationship depth / multi-threading
- service issues / recovery signals
- quote or opportunity activity
- customer momentum over time
- engagement with strategic contacts
- inactivity or shrinking share of wallet

Requirements:
1. Start by proposing the scoring model before writing code.
2. Explain the logic in plain English so a rep or leader can understand it.
3. Include a 0–100 score scale with clear score bands (for example: 0–25 risk, 26–50 stable, 51–75 growth, 76–100 high expansion opportunity).
4. Include "why this score" explanations showing the top drivers for each account.
5. Recommend how the score should appear in the UI:
   - account list view
   - account detail page
   - leader dashboard
   - alerts / task list
6. Recommend whether the score should be:
   - mostly rules-based for MVP
   - or hybrid rules + AI later
7. Keep the first version simple, transparent, and trustworthy.
8. Make sure the feature helps action, not just reporting.
9. Do not code yet. First provide the design and implementation plan.

Please return:
A. Score concept
B. Signal model
C. Score band definitions
D. UI placement recommendations
E. MVP vs later version
F. Data needed from the app
G. Risks / edge cases
H. Step-by-step implementation plan
